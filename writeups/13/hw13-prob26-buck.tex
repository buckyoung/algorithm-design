\begin{homeworkProblem}
\centerline{\textbf{Problem 26}}
\leavevmode
\\
\textbf{Input:} A two-dimensional array $R$ of non-negative integers and an integer $k$. The value $R_{t,\ p}$ gives the number of users requesting page $p$ at time $t$. The server can broadcast at most $k$ times.
\\ \\ \textbf{Output:} Pick the $k$ times to broadcast, and the pages to broadcast at those $k$ times, in order to minimize the total time (over all requests) that requests/users have to wait in order to have their requests satisfied.
\\ \\ \textbf{The Tree:} The tree for this problem will go down level by level based on if pages should broadcast at time $level$. For two pages, $A$ and $B$, we will create a new leaf for A, B, both A and B, and neither A nor B. For three pages, $A$ and $B$ and $C$, we will create a new leaf for A, B, C, A and B, A and C, B and C, all A B and C, and none A B nor C. Thus, for two pages, each node will have a branching factor of 4 -- and for three pages, each node will have a branching factor of 8. We can extend this to say that for $x$ number of pages, the branching factor is $2^x$.
\\ \\ \textbf{Pruning Rules:} 
\\ 1) If (at any node) the total number of broadcasts across all pages is greater than $k$, prune that node.
\\ 2) If total wait time is the same at any two nodes in the same level, keep the node with the fewer total broadcasts across all pages.
\\ \\ \textbf{Algorithm:} We did not develop a solution to this problem.
\end{homeworkProblem}
